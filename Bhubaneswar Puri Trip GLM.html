Overview

Why This Trip?

Bhubaneswar is the "Temple City of India" — home to over 700 ancient temples spanning a thousand years of Kalinga architecture. Puri, just 60 km away, is one of Hinduism's four sacred Char Dham pilgrimage sites, with a beach that belongs equally to pilgrims and sunset-chasers.

Together, these cities offer something rare: a travel experience that is spiritually rich, architecturally stunning, genuinely uncrowded in most spots, and remarkably affordable. The local Odia cuisine — dalma, chhena poda, machha besara — is a world away from typical Indian restaurant food.

This plan balances iconic landmarks with hidden gems, keeps you well-fed on local flavors, and stays comfortably within $500 for one person.

The Journey

Day-by-Day Itinerary

01
Arrival & Temple Quarter
Bhubaneswar — Old Town Circuit
~$22 / ₹1,830
Morning
Lingaraj Temple Free
The crown jewel of Bhubaneswar's temples, dating to the 11th century. Dedicated to Lord Shiva as Harihara (half Shiva, half Vishnu). The towering 55m deul is covered in intricate carvings. Non-Hindus can view from an elevated platform outside — still breathtaking. Arrive by 7 AM to avoid crowds and catch the morning aarti glow.
⏰ 6:00 AM – 9:00 AM 💰 Free entry Lingaraj Temple on Maps
Mid-Day
Lunch at Dalma Restaurant
The best place in Bhubaneswar to try authentic Odia thali. Their signature dalma (dal cooked with vegetables and raw banana) is legendary. Order the veg thali with chhena poda for dessert. Clean, air-conditioned, and very affordable.
💰 ~₹200 ($2.40) Dalma on Maps
Afternoon
Mukteshwar & Rajarani Temples Hidden Gem Free
Walk 10 minutes from Lingaraj to Mukteshwar — a compact 10th-century gem with Odisha's most beautiful torana (arched gateway). The carved dwarfs, celestial maidens, and lion motifs are exquisite. Then take an auto to Rajarani Temple, known as the "love temple" for its erotic carvings. Set in a peaceful garden, it's often uncrowded. The laterite stone glows golden at this hour.
⏰ 1:00 PM – 4:00 PM 💰 Free 🚗 Auto: ₹50 Mukteshwar on Maps
Evening
Dinner at Truptee
A Bhubaneswar institution since 1962. Simple vegetarian Odia food — try the aloo dum, besara, and poori combo. No frills, just honest flavor. Very popular with locals.
💰 ~₹150 ($1.80) Truptee on Maps
Night
Ekamra Haat Optional Free
An open-air crafts market designed like a traditional Odia village. Handloom sarees, silver filigree, palm leaf paintings, and pipili appliqué work. Great for browsing even if you don't buy. Live Odissi music some evenings.
⏰ Closes 9 PM Ekamra Haat on Maps
💡 Transport Tip

From the railway station to Old Town (Lingaraj area), take a shared auto for ₹20 or a dedicated auto for ₹60–80. The temple area is walkable once you're there. Ola/Uber are also available in Bhubaneswar.

02
Caves, Stupas & Tribal Art
Bhubaneswar — Western Circuit
~$30 / ₹2,490
Morning
Khandagiri & Udayagiri Caves
Two hills facing each other, carved with 2nd-century BC Jain rock-cut caves. Udayagiri has the famous Rani Gumpha (Queen's Cave) with double-story carvings of musicians, dancers, and royal processions. Khandagiri offers a short hike to the top with panoramic views of Bhubaneswar. Go early — by 9 AM it gets hot.
⏰ 7:00 AM – 10:30 AM 💰 ₹25 each hill ($0.60 total) 🚗 Auto round trip: ₹300 Caves on Maps
Mid-Day
Lunch at Bhatu Bhati
A no-nonsense local eatery near Khandagiri. Serves hot dalma, rice, and fish curry on stainless steel thalis. The kind of place where auto drivers eat — which means it's good and cheap.
💰 ~₹120 ($1.45) Bhatu Bhati on Maps
Afternoon
Dhauli Shanti Stupa Hidden Gem
The peace pagoda built by Japanese monks atop Dhauli Hill — the exact site where Emperor Ashoka fought the Kalinga War in 261 BC, then embraced Buddhism. The white stupa against green hills with the Daya River below is deeply moving. The rock edicts of Ashoka are at the base. Almost zero foreign tourists here.
⏰ 1:30 PM – 3:30 PM 💰 Free 🚗 Auto from Khandagiri: ₹150 Dhauli on Maps
Late Aft.
Odisha State Museum Free
Back in the city, spend an hour here. Excellent collection of Jain and Buddhist sculptures, palm leaf manuscripts, tribal artifacts, and traditional Odia paintings. The epigraphy gallery alone is worth the visit. Air-conditioned — a nice break from the heat.
⏰ 4:00 PM – 5:30 PM 💰 Free (₹20 camera fee) Museum on Maps
Evening
Dinner at Odisha Hotel
Near the museum area. Try their mutton curry (mansha tarkari) with steamed rice — a proper Odia non-veg meal. Also good: chungdi malai (prawn in coconut milk).
💰 ~₹250 ($3) Odisha Hotel on Maps
💡 Transport Tip

Hire an auto for the full day (Khandagiri → Dhauli → Museum → Hotel) for ₹500–600. Negotiate upfront. Carry water — the caves have almost no shade. Wear shoes you can remove easily for temple entry.

03
Konark Sun Temple & Transfer to Puri
Bhubaneswar → Konark → Puri
~$42 / ₹3,490
Morning
Konark Sun Temple (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
The 13th-century masterpiece shaped as a colossal chariot of the Sun God, with 24 carved stone wheels and seven horses. Every inch is covered in sculpture — erotic art, musicians, animals, deities. The wheels function as sundials. Arrive by 7 AM for golden light on the east-facing temple and near-empty grounds. This is one of India's greatest monuments.
⏰ 6:30 AM – 10:00 AM 💰 ₹40 ($0.50) 🚗 Bus from Bhubaneswar: ₹80 / Shared cab: ₹200 Konark on Maps
Mid-Day
Lunch at Priya Restaurant, Konark
Simple, clean, and right near the temple. Good thali options and cold beverages after a hot morning. Not fancy — just reliable.
💰 ~₹180 ($2.15) Priya on Maps
Afternoon
Konark Beach & Chandrabhaga River Hidden Gem Free
Most tourists leave Konark after the temple. Don't. Walk 3 km to Chandrabhaga beach — a pristine, nearly empty stretch of sand where the Konark river meets the sea. Locals come here for a sacred dip during the annual Magha Saptami fair. The sunset here is unreal. Then take a bus or cab to Puri (35 km, 45 min).
⏰ 1:00 PM – 4:00 PM 💰 Free 🚗 Konark → Puri bus: ₹50 / Cab: ₹400 Chandrabhaga on Maps
Evening
Check-in & Puri Beach Walk Free
Check into your Puri hotel. Then walk the beach at sunset — the stretch from Chakratirtha to Swargadwar is vibrant with fishermen, pilgrims, sand artists, and food stalls. Try the fresh roasted corn or a tender coconut.
💰 Hotel: ₹1,200 ($14) Puri Beach on Maps
Dinner
Dinner at Chung Wah
Surprisingly excellent Chinese-Indian food in Puri. A local favorite for decades. Try the chilli chicken and fried rice. A break from Odia food after two days.
💰 ~₹250 ($3) Chung Wah on Maps
💡 Transport Tip

The most economical route: OSRTC bus from Bhubaneswar to Konark (₹80, 1.5 hrs), then another bus from Konark to Puri (₹50, 45 min). If you split a cab with other tourists at Konark, it's ₹150–200 per person. Book your Puri hotel in advance during festival season (June–July).

04
Sacred Puri & Artist Village
Puri — Jagannath, Raghurajpur & Market
~$25 / ₹2,075
Morning
Jagannath Temple Free
One of the four Char Dham — the abode of Lord Jagannath (Krishna). The massive 12th-century temple complex is alive with devotion, color, and incense. Non-Hindus cannot enter the inner sanctum but can view from the Raghunandan Library rooftop nearby (₹20 tip) for a stunning panoramic view. If you're Hindu, go inside early — the morning darshan is most peaceful. The kitchen here feeds 10,000+ people daily.
⏰ 6:00 AM – 9:00 AM 💰 Free Jagannath Temple on Maps
Mid-Day
Temple Mahaprasad (Lunch)
The sacred food of Jagannath, cooked in the world's largest kitchen. You can get mahaprasad at the Ananda Bazaar inside the temple complex (Hindus only) or from authorized stalls outside. Rice, dal, vegetables, and a unique sweet — all cooked in earthen pots over wood fire. An experience, not just a meal.
💰 ~₹100–150 ($1.20–1.80)
Afternoon
Raghurajpur Heritage Village Hidden Gem
Just 12 km from Puri, this is Odisha's most beautiful artisan village. Every house is decorated with pattachitra (traditional cloth-based scroll painting). Watch artists paint intricate mythological scenes using natural colors. You can buy directly from them — a pattachitra starts at ₹500. The village also produces palm leaf engravings, paper mache masks, and stone carvings. This is the most culturally rich experience of the entire trip.
⏰ 1:30 PM – 4:30 PM 💰 Free entry (buy art if you wish) 🚗 Auto round trip: ₹300 Raghurajpur on Maps
Evening
Puri Market (Bada Danda) & Dinner
Walk the Grand Road leading to the temple — lined with shops selling religious souvenirs, seashell crafts, and the famous Puri rasagola (soft spongy cottage cheese balls in sugar syrup). Pick up a tin from Bhikari Halwai or Pahala rasagola stalls. For dinner, grab seafood at one of the beach shacks — try the machha besara (fish in mustard sauce).
💰 Dinner ~₹200 ($2.40) + Snacks ~₹100 Bada Danda on Maps
Night
Beach Stargazing Optional Free
Puri's beach is mostly free of light pollution toward the fishing village end. Walk 10 minutes past the main stretch, sit on the sand, and enjoy one of the clearest night skies you'll find on India's east coast.
💰 Free
💡 Transport Tip

Negotiate the Raghurajpur auto to ₹250–300 round trip with 1 hour wait time. Inside Jagannath Temple, you'll need to remove shoes and leather items. Keep belongings in the cloakroom (₹10). Beware of pandas (temple priests) aggressively demanding donations — a polite but firm "no" works.

05
Sunrise, Lake & Departure
Puri — Final Morning & Return to Bhubaneswar
~$18 / ₹1,495
Dawn
Puri Beach Sunrise Free
Wake up at 5:15 AM and walk to the beach. Puri faces east, and the sunrise over the Bay of Bengal is spectacular — the sky turns coral, amber, then gold. Fishermen haul in their nets, seagulls wheel overhead, and the temple spire catches the first light. This single moment encapsulates everything beautiful about Puri.
⏰ 5:30 AM – 6:30 AM 💰 Free
Morning
Gundicha Temple & Narendra Tank Hidden Gem Free
Gundicha Temple is where Lord Jagannath stays during the famous Rath Yatra. It's just 2 km from the main temple but sees a fraction of the visitors — serene, tree-shaded, with beautiful gardens. Then walk to Narendra Tank, a massive sacred pond where the Chandan Yatra (boat festival) happens. The black stone structure in the center is photogenic. Both are free and take under 1.5 hours combined.
⏰ 7:00 AM – 8:30 AM 💰 Free Gundicha Temple on Maps
Breakfast
Breakfast at a Beach Shack
Aloor dom (spicy potato curry) with luchi (fried bread) and a cup of chai, sitting on a plastic chair with sand between your toes. Simple, perfect.
💰 ~₹80 ($1)
Late Morn.
Sudarshan Craft Museum Free
A small but excellent museum showcasing Odisha's craft traditions — stone carving, woodwork, metal work, and tribal art. The outdoor section has full-scale replicas of temple sculptures. Often missed by tourists because it's tucked away near the beach.
⏰ 9:30 AM – 10:30 AM 💰 Free Sudarshan Museum on Maps
Afternoon
Return to Bhubaneswar & Departure
Take the Puri–Bhubaneswar Passenger Train (₹50, 1.5 hrs) or an OSRTC bus (₹80, 2 hrs). The train is faster and more comfortable. If your flight/train is in the evening, spend your last hours at Odisha State Museum (if missed on Day 2) or grab a final meal at Dalma before heading to the airport. Biju Patnaik Airport is 15 min from the city center by cab (₹300).
💰 Train: ₹50 ($0.60) + Airport cab: ₹300 ($3.60) 💰 Final meal: ~₹200 ($2.40) Airport on Maps
💡 Transport Tip

Book the Puri–Bhubaneswar train ticket a day in advance via IRCTC or at the Puri station counter. Unreserved general tickets are always available but seats aren't guaranteed. For the airport, pre-book an Ola or negotiate with a fixed-rate cab stand near the station to avoid haggling.

Essential Knowledge

Travel Tips & Mistakes to Avoid

Things I wish someone had told me before my first Odisha trip.

🌤

Best Time to Visit

  • October–March: Pleasant weather, 20–30°C
  • June–July: Rath Yatra (massive crowds)
  • April–May: Very hot (40°C+), avoid
  • Aug–Sep: Rainy but lush and green
⚠️

Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't pay "donation" at temples — it's optional
  • Don't skip Raghurajpur — it's the trip's highlight
  • Don't take a cab from the airport without fixing price
  • Don't wear shorts at Jagannath or Lingaraj temples
  • Don't drink tap water — stick to bottled
🛡

Safety Tips

  • Odisha is very safe — violent crime is rare
  • Beach currents can be strong — don't swim far
  • Keep phone charged — power cuts happen
  • Carry a photocopy of your ID
  • Use official prepaid auto stands at railway stations
💸

Saving Money

  • Shared autos are 1/5th the cost of private ones
  • OSRTC buses are clean and reliable
  • Most temples are free — don't pay "guides"
  • Eat at local joints, not hotel restaurants
  • Bottled water: buy 1L bottles (₹20) not small ones
📱

Connectivity

  • Jio has the best network in both cities
  • Get a local SIM if staying 5+ days
  • Free WiFi at most budget hotels (unreliable)
  • Download offline Google Maps for both cities
  • Ola and Uber work in Bhubaneswar, limited in Puri
🍛

Must-Try Foods

  • Dalma — the signature Odia dish
  • Chhena Poda — baked cottage cheese "cake"
  • Machha Besara — fish in mustard gravy
  • Rasagola — Puri's version is different (softer)
  • Chungdi Malai — prawns in coconut milk
